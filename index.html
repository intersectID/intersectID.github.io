INTERSECT 2024

INTERSECT 2024 is an interdisciplinary symposium presenting the brightest minds in academia, research, industry, and policy-making. This year, we are excited to host the event at Stanford University, the birthplace of Silicon Valley and the epicenter of technological innovation, although most programs will run virtually on August 2nd, 2024. This year's theme is "Indonesia at the Crossroad of Technology, Sustainability, and Society", underscoring the urgent needs for collaborative effort to address the challenges and opportunities in Indonesia's sustainable development.

We invite academics, researchers, practitioners, and policymakers to join us and synthesize robust policy recommendations and academic blueprints for Indonesian pathways to sustainable development. Our ultimate goal is to create a dynamic network that strengthens Indonesia's approach to practical, real-world challenges through technological and scientific innovation. We seek to foster collaborations that leverage advancements in technology and science to strengthen a sustainability-focused economy, underpinned by AI, robotics, and other technological breakthroughs.

Call for Submissions

We are seeking extended abstract or full-paper contributions that not only resonate with the theme but also extend the boundaries in sustainability, economic development, and technological innovation. The topics include, but are not limited to:
  • Renewable Energy Technologies and Markets: Advancements in solar, wind, and nuclear energy, economic impacts, market trends, and financing models.
  • Sustainable Urban Development and Smart Cities: Sustainable urban planning, smart infrastructure, and economic models for smart city development.
  • Environmental Economics and Climate Policy: Economics of environmental policies, market-based management strategies, and financial aspects of climate change mitigation.
  • Community Engagement and Volunteering in Sustainability: Grassroots movements, community-driven initiatives, and the role of volunteering in sustainability projects.
  • Innovations in Sustainable Smart Cities and Intelligent Transportation: Exploring the design and impact of sustainable urban planning, smart infrastructure, and electric and autonomous vehicles.
  • Digitalization in Green Supply Chains: Digital technologies in sustainable supply chains and impacts of big data in environmental economics.
  • Entrepreneurship in Green Technology and Sustainability: Startup ecosystems, venture capital in sustainability, and circular economy innovations.
  • AI, Robotics, and Automation in Sustainability: Robotics in sustainable manufacturing, waste management, and agricultural efficiency.
  • Bioengineering, Biotechnology, and Sustainable Healthcare: Intersection of bioengineering and biotechnology with healthcare practices aligned with SDGs.
Submissions will be reviewed by the program committees. The accepted papers will be published in the symposium proceedings and the authors are invited to present their work in the symposium remotely (synchronous). We will award the best (full) paper for each track and the best overall presentation.

We use OpenReview to handle submission processes. For more information about the submission, please visit the submission page.

Young Researcher Competition (For High School Students)

We are also hosting a short research paper competition for high school students. The competition is open to all high school students in Indonesia. The topic of the paper should be related to the symposium theme. The short paper should be written in English and follow standard academic writing guidelines. The paper should be 5-8 pages, excluding references. All works must be original and 95% of the content must be written by the student/student team (up to 3 students). To be fully considered, the paper must be submitted by a supervising teacher or school representative.

The finalists will be invited to present their work remotely in the symposium. The top three winners will receive a certificate and cash prize. For more information about the competition, please visit the Young Researcher Competition page.
